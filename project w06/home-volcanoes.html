Our Mission

Ecuador is home to some of the most magnificent and active volcanoes in the world. From snow-capped peaks to simmering craters, each volcano holds a story of ancient power and natural wonder. Our mission is to guide you through these geological marvels — to understand their origins, appreciate their beauty, and respect the powerful forces that shape our planet. Join us as we explore the landscapes that define Ecuador’s unique identity.

What Will You Discover?

Chimborazo is taller in absolute height (6,263 m), but Cotopaxi (5,897 m) is one of the highest active volcanoes in the world.

Cotopaxi and Tungurahua are among Ecuador’s active volcanoes, with Cotopaxi erupting as recently as 2023.

Ecuador's volcanoes are part of the Ring of Fire and are located near the equator, combining tropical and high-altitude environments.

It used to be possible, but due to its active status and safety concerns, hiking is now restricted or prohibited depending on activity levels.

Local legends say that mountains like Tungurahua and Chimborazo are living spirits, often depicted in romantic or warrior tales.

They create fertile soils, shape landscapes, but also pose threats to local communities due to ashfall and lava flows.
